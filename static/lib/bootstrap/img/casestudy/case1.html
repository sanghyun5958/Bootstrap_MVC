Usaha Wanita Mobile Service

A mobile service developed by Cherie Blair for women entrepreneurs who need information to help guide them through their efforts to lower their costs and expand their enterprises.

Details

• ‘Usaha Wanita’ means ‘Business Women’ in Indonesian language Bahasa
• Operating Country: Indonesia since 2012 
• In partnership with: ExxonMobil Foundation, Nokia and Indosat
• Based on in-depth research to determine the key barriers facing women entrepreneurs in Indonesia
• Developed as a teaching tool to help women entrepreneurs overcome specific challenges by providing essential business tips and market information
• Also provide hands-on business development support with Mercy Corps Indonesia: delivered business capacity building training for 2,000 women

Results

• Reached more than 14,000 women in Indonesia (2015)
• The service was tailored and delivered to women entrepreneurs in Nigeria and Tanzania – reached more than 100,000 women in total
• Won the Best Consumer Innovation Award at the Global Telecoms Business 2013 Awards
• 90% of subscribers felt the service gave them ‘practical guidance’ on making their businesses grow and offered an ‘inexpensive way to become better informed business women’

More information from different sources

- The need for this initiative arose out of a GSMA report highlighting a wide gender gap in mobile phone ownership in developing and emerging economies
- The service uses mobile phone technology to provide essential business information in three areas: access to finance, retail channel participation, and customized applications to scale up businesses
- Aim: to build “tailored mobile value-added services” for female entrepreneurs in Indonesia by identifying challenges faced by women in this economy and addressing them through these value-added services. In particular, the service targets female entrepreneurs running or starting micro-businesses
- An ExxonMobil Foundation grant of US$1.5 million to the Cherie Blair Foundation for Women supported the initial research and development of the mobile phone service, and will help support basic business skills training to women in Indonesia
- The tailored mobile application provided is the result of research findings from a study entitled Mobile Value Added Services (MWAS): A Business Growth Opportunity for Women Entrepreneurs. The study concludes that the growing penetration of mobile phones in developing markets presents a significant opportunity to women entrepreneurs who want to develop their microbusinesses
- Usaha Wanita complements Indosat’s women-focused mobile initiatives including the award-winning Info Wanita (Information for Women), which provides important information on financial management, life skills, health and childcare delivered through SMS; and Hebat Keluarga (Great Family), a dedicated offering targeting stay-at-home mums, with features such as a family-friendly tariff and a family finder geo-location tracker
- Usaha Wanita Application: a mobile application specifically for women entrepreneurs that is made accessible through the Nokia Life + web-app. This service is targets women owners of small and medium-sized business or women starting their business
- Through Nokia Life+, Usaha Wanita will give thousands of women micro-entrepreneurs useful information in their local language on topics such as business management, banking, how to get a loan, customer service and marketing
 - Nokia Life+ is supported across a range of basic Nokia handsets, Series 40 phones and the Asha smartphones, including the recently launched Nokia Asha 308 and Nokia Asha 309. - The free Nokia Life+ web application can be downloaded from the Nokia Store, or it can be accessed directly through the new and improved Nokia Xpress Browser, which compresses data by up to 90%, resulting in faster and more affordable mobile Internet access
- Nokia teamed up with Indosat to create and deliver a special service called ‘Info Wanita’ on its Nokia Life services platform. ‘Info Wanita’ (Information for Women) is available free for one year to Indosat subscribers on selected Nokia Asha devices
- Info Wanita delivers information on themes such as Small Business Management, Financial Management, Life Skills, Health and Childcare. The content was developed by Nokia in partnership with the Foundation for Social Change, a non-profit organisation focused on empowering women and girls in developing countries
- Info Wanita can be activated easily from within the Nokia Life services menu that comes pre-embedded in the Nokia Asha phones
- Through Info Wanita and Usaha Wanita services, Indonesian women can access a variety of information on topics ranging from business and finance, health, family / children, financial management, and communication skills, to human resource development. Approximately 20,000 women in Indonesia have utilized information from these two services.
