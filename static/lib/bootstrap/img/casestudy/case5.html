SheTrades (ITC)

• SheTrades is an initiative on women and trade launched by the International Trade Centre (ITC) in 2015 which aims to connect one million women entrepreneurs to markets by 2020.

• Its web and mobile applications provide a global platform that brings together thousands of women owned enterprises to showcase their offerings from a variety of sectors ranging from agriculture to IT services.

Details

• SheTrades provides women entrepreneurs across the world a unique platform to connect to markets. Through the SheTrades mobile app and a website, women entrepreneurs can share information about their companies, promote their products and services, enable them to find new business and partnership opportunities, and match them with companies that have complimentary interests.
• It also helps corporations to include more women entrepreneurs in their supply chains. As a SheTrades member, companies have full access to women entrepreneurs’ profiles and company information. Through well- customized filters, companies can identify women entrepreneurs that could meet their sourcing requirements.
• Its core features include:
    o SME Trade Academy : a distance learning programme offering a series of online courses in support of skills development and links to knowledge resources on a range of topics in trade and business. Access to ‘Trade Tools’ :
    o a suite of online tools to make global trade more transparent and facilitate access to new markets, including Trade Map, Market Access Map, Investment Map, Standards Map, Procurement Map and Trade Competitiveness Map.
   o Access to ITC’s Market Analysis Tools is entirely free of charge thanks to support from the World Bank, the European Commission and funders of the ITC's Trust Fund. 

Results

• According to a user survey, ITC tools contributed to US$ 126 million in exports and are deemed particularly instrumental to users in least developed countries, where local sources of trade intelligence are often unavailable.
• Until now, several hundred women entrepreneurs and women-owned businesses have registered on the beta version of SheTrades since December 2015.
